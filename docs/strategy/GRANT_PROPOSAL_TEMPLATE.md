**Organization**: Technological Foods

## Executive Summary

We propose to deploy **solar-powered, offline-first hydroponic systems** in 3 remote communities, providing sustainable food production, economic opportunity, and technology education. Each system costs $600, produces $2,000+ in annual value, and operates completely off-grid with no internet dependency.

**Impact**: 300 people gain access to fresh vegetables, 6 full-time jobs created, 1,500 kg of food produced in first 6 months.

## Implementation Plan

### Phase 1: Preparation (Month 1)

#### Community Engagement
- Community meetings to introduce project
- Site assessments (water, power, space)
- Select 2-3 community technicians per site
- Form management committees

#### Procurement
- Order components (bulk purchase for cost savings)
- Source local materials (PVC pipes, tanks)
- Secure solar panels and batteries
- Prepare training materials

**Deliverable**: 3 communities ready, materials on-site

---

### Phase 2: Installation & Training (Month 2)

#### Week 1: Installation
- Assemble systems with community participation
- Install solar power systems
- Set up sensors and monitoring
- Configure local database and SMS alerts

#### Week 2: Basic Training (All Community Members)
- Introduction to hydroponics
- Dashboard navigation (SMS commands)
- Alert interpretation
- Basic troubleshooting

#### Week 3: Technical Training (Community Technicians)
- Sensor calibration
- Nutrient management
- pH/EC balancing
- System maintenance
- Data analysis

**Deliverable**: 3 operational systems, 30+ people trained
